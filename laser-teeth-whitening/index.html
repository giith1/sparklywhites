Official FAQs
Is laser teeth whitening safe for everyone?

Laser teeth whitening is generally safe when performed by a qualified professional. However, it may not be suitable for everyone. Pregnant women, individuals with severe gum disease, tooth decay, or extremely sensitive teeth should avoid the procedure until these issues are addressed. Always consult with your dentist to ensure you’re a good candidate.

Are there any side effects of laser teeth whitening?

Yes, some common side effects include temporary tooth sensitivity and mild gum irritation. These effects usually subside within a few days. In rare cases, overuse or improper application can lead to enamel damage. Following post-treatment instructions helps minimize risks.

How does laser teeth whitening compare to other whitening methods in terms of safety?

Laser teeth whitening is considered safer and more controlled than over-the-counter whitening products. It is performed under professional supervision, reducing the risk of misuse. However, it is more intense than home remedies or custom trays, which might not suit individuals with sensitivity issues.

What precautions should I take before undergoing laser teeth whitening?

To prepare for laser teeth whitening, ensure your teeth and gums are healthy. Schedule a dental check-up to address cavities or gum inflammation. Avoid highly acidic or pigmented foods leading up to the procedure, as they can exacerbate sensitivity.

How can I maintain my teeth’s safety after laser whitening?

Post-treatment, avoid staining foods like coffee, tea, and red wine for at least 48 hours. Use a toothpaste designed for sensitive teeth and avoid smoking to prevent discoloration. Regular dental visits and proper oral hygiene are key to maintaining results without compromising oral health.
